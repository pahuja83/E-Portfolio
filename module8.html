MODULE 8

RESEARCH METHODS AND PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE

Learning Outcomes:

Unit-1: Scientific Investigation and Ethics
1. Differentiate between inductive and deductive reasoning.
2. Understand why ethics are important and how they may relate to your area of research and your professional practice.

  • CollaborationDiscussion-1-Initial-Post
  • Unit1-Reasoning-quiz

    • Unit-2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal
    1. Examine the characteristics that make up a suitable research topic.
    2. Explore rational and creative methods for formulating a research idea.
    3. Identify means of transforming research ideas into crafted research questions and proposal.
    4. Conduct a literature search, critique the literature and to present a literature review.

  • CollaborationDiscussion-1-Peer-Reviews

    • Unit-3: Research Methods
    1. The concept of exploratory and descriptive research designs.
    2. Different research methods - quantitative, qualitative and mixed method research.
    3. Data collection methods related to each method of research.

  • CollaborationDiscussion-1-SummaryPost

    • Unit-4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations
    1. How to carry out each of these data collection methods.
    2. Which method would be suitable for the investigation.
    3. Type of data one would obtain.

  • Literature-Review-Outline

    • Unit-5: Interviews and Survey Methods
    1. How interviews and surveys fit into research and whether either or both of these might be useful for the investigation.
    2. How pre- and post-testing can be used in research.

    Unit-6: Questionnaire Design
    1. Difference between a good questionnaire and a poor one.
    2. Design a questionnaire to obtain the responses needed for the investigation
    3. How the data obtained can be analyzed.

    Unit-7: Validity and Generalisability in Research
    1. Understand of how the concepts of validity, generalisability and reliability affect the investigation and the design of the research method.
    2. How to analyse and present the results obtained from the investigation and how they will enable to answer the research question.

    • Unit-8:Inferential Statistics
    1. Different levels of measurement.
    2. Produce measures of location and spread.
    3. Perform appropriate hypothesis tests.
